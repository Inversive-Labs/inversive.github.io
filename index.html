A b o u t

Inversive Labs is a boutique R&D security firm specializing in low-level security research and advanced tooling development. We operate at the intersection of vulnerability research and tool engineering, focusing on the fundamental challenges that require deep technical expertise.

We focus on understanding systems at their lowest levels, analyzing bytecode, tracing execution flows, and building custom tools. This attention to detail is what makes us different, emphasizing rigorous analysis and technical excellence.

Founded by researchers with deep expertise in offensive security, threat intelligence, and smart contract security, Inversive Labs brings a unique perspective to security challenges. We thrive on technically complex problems that require innovative solutions.

T e a m

Sergio Corrales

Co-Founder and Security Researcher

Sergio Corrales is an experienced security researcher and developer who previously founded a blockchain security company, with deep expertise in smart contract security, static analysis tooling, and blockchain protocol development.

His technical contributions span multiple blockchain ecosystems including EVM and Non-EVM, with notable work on security audits for high-profile clients, and custom security tooling such as Solana Static Analyzer.

In addition, Sergio has collaborated with R55, an experimental Ethereum Execution Environment that integrates RISCV smart contracts alongside traditional EVM smart contracts.

Sergio has developed custom static analysis detectors for Aderyn (winning a Cyfrin hackathon), built Solana indexers and DEX integrations, and conducted security assessments across 25+ private audits finding over 170 vulnerabilities.

His research focus on fuzzing campaigns, invariant testing, and low-level vulnerability analysis has uncovered critical issues in DeFi protocols, staking systems, and cross-chain infrastructure.

Ignacio Sanmillan

Co-Founder and Security Researcher

Ignacio Sanmillan is an experienced security researcher with extensive experience in vulnerability research across browsers, IoT, and mobile platforms, with a proven track record of discovering critical vulnerabilities in major systems including Safari, Chrome, and iOS components.

His expertise spans advanced exploitation techniques, reverse engineering, and fuzzing methodologies, with notable contributions to browser security through active participation in Apple Security Bounty and Google Chrome VRP programs.

Ignacio has successfully led research teams focused on Safari sandbox escapes and user-space iOS vulnerability discovery. His technical contributions include developing custom fuzzing tools and conducting deep research on various topics, including Non-EVM runtime implementations, and ELF binary instrumentation and protection techniques.

He has authored multiple security advisories and several critical WebKit vulnerabilities. With a background in APT tradecraft analysis and state-nexus threat intelligence, Ignacio has extensive experience in malware reverse engineering, supply-chain compromise, and state-sponsored cybersecurity operations.

His work has been featured in prominent security conferences including VirusBulletin and r2con. He maintains active open-source contributions to radare2 and various security research projects.
