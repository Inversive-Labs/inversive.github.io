Our Team

Sergio Corrales

Co-Founder and Security Researcher

Sergio Corrales is a security researcher and developer specializing in smart contract security, static analysis tooling, and blockchain protocol development across EVM and Non-EVM ecosystems.

He has conducted 25+ private security audits, discovering over 170 vulnerabilities in DeFi protocols, staking systems, and cross-chain infrastructure. His tooling contributions include the Solana Static Analyzer and custom detectors for Aderyn (winning a Cyfrin hackathon).

Sergio's research focuses on fuzzing campaigns, invariant testing, and low-level vulnerability analysis. He has also collaborated on R55, an experimental Ethereum Execution Environment integrating RISCV smart contracts alongside traditional EVM contracts.

Ignacio Sanmillan

Co-Founder and Security Researcher

Ignacio Sanmillan is a security researcher previously specializing in vulnerability discovery across browsers, and mobile platforms. He has identified critical vulnerabilities in Safari, Chrome, and iOS components through active participation in Apple Security Bounty and Google Chrome VRP programs.

His technical work spans advanced binary exploitation, reverse engineering, and custom fuzzer development. Ignacio has led technical teams on browser sandbox escape vulnerability research, and has also contributed to projects involving Non-EVM runtime analysis and ELF binary instrumentation.

His work has been featured at conferences such as VirusBulletin or R2Con, and his research published by security communities such as Tmp.Out. He maintains active contributions to radare2 and other open-source security projects.
